The interactive India map at `/explore` is the primary way to discover Virasat's cultural content. This guide walks you through every part of the map interface.

<Note>
As you scroll down the page, the map card smoothly expands from a rounded glass card (with 40px border radius and slight margin) to a full-screen panel that spans the entire viewport. This fluid animation is driven by Framer Motion scroll transforms — no jarring page transitions.
</Note>

## Opening the map

Navigate to `/explore` in the app. The page loads with a large hero section:

- The **BHARAT — THE LAND OF TIMELESS HERITAGE** title fills the top of the screen
- Below it, a **3D Heritage Showcase** displays six curated Hasta Mudra models (Alapadma, Anjali, Pataka, Garuda, Mayura, Chakra) that you can interact with
- Scrolling down reveals the interactive India map card

## Regional filter tabs

Across the top of the map panel, a pill-shaped tab bar shows the 7 geographic regions of India:

- **NORTH** — Jammu & Kashmir, Himachal Pradesh, Punjab, Uttarakhand, Haryana, Delhi, Uttar Pradesh, Chandigarh, Ladakh
- **SOUTH** — Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Karnataka, Kerala, Tamil Nadu, Puducherry, Andaman & Nicobar, Lakshadweep
- **EAST** — Bihar, Jharkhand, West Bengal, Odisha
- **WEST** — Rajasthan, Gujarat, Maharashtra, Goa, Dadra & Nagar Haveli, Daman & Diu
- **CENTRAL** — Madhya Pradesh, Chhattisgarh
- **NORTH EAST** — Sikkim, Assam, Arunachal Pradesh, Nagaland, Manipur, Mizoram, Tripura, Meghalaya
- **UNION TERRITORIES** — UI filter grouping for all union territories across the geographic regions above

Clicking a region tab highlights the states in that region at full opacity and fades all other states to lower opacity.

## Navigating to a state

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select a region">
    Click one of the 7 region filter tabs to zoom into that part of India. States in the region are highlighted.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click a state shape">
    Click any highlighted state on the map. The map panel expands to fill the full viewport height.
  </Step>
  <Step title="View the cultural detail panel">
    A state detail panel slides in from the right on desktop, or slides up from the bottom on mobile. It shows the state's monuments, art forms, festivals, museums, and more.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Explore monuments on the map">
    When a state is active, monument POI markers appear on the map at their geographic coordinates. Click a marker to scroll directly to that monument in the detail panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go back">
    Click the **×** button in the top-right of the state detail panel, or click anywhere on the visible map area outside the panel, to return to the region view. Click a region tab again (or navigate away) to return to the full India view.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## High contrast mode

In the top-right corner of the map toolbar, next to the regional filter tabs, there is an **Eye** icon button. Clicking it toggles **High Contrast** color mode:

- **Default mode** — colorful fill palette (sky blue, orange, green, purple, yellow, pink, teal) with white state borders
- **High Contrast mode** — all states fill to solid black with bright yellow borders; POI markers turn white

This mode is useful for accessibility and for focusing on geographic boundaries without the distraction of color.

## URL deep-linking

<Tip>
You can navigate directly to any state's detail panel by visiting `/explore/StateName` — for example, `/explore/Rajasthan` or `/explore/Tamil%20Nadu`. The map loads, selects the state automatically, and opens its cultural panel. Share this URL with others to link directly to a specific state.
</Tip>

The URL also updates automatically when you select a state through the map UI, so your browser history reflects the navigation path.
