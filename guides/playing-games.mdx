Virasat includes 7 educational mini-games accessible at `/games`. Each game tests a different aspect of knowledge about Indian states, culture, and heritage.

When you first open `/games`, you are prompted to **enter your name**. This name is used on the downloadable participation certificate you receive after completing a game. After submitting your name, the full game arcade is shown — click any game card to begin.

<Tip>
On mobile, all drag-and-drop games support a **tap-to-select** alternative. Tap a state chip or item to select it (it highlights in amber), then tap the target zone to place it. No dragging required.
</Tip>

## Games

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="State on Map — drag-and-drop map game">
    **Difficulty:** Easy

    Place Indian state names onto their correct locations on a D3-rendered India map.

    **How to play:**

    1. 10 random states are selected from the full list at the start of each round.
    2. State name chips appear in a row below the map. Drag a chip from the bottom strip and drop it onto the correct state shape on the map.
    3. On mobile, tap a chip to select it, then tap the correct state shape on the map.
    4. A correct drop turns the chip **green**; a wrong drop turns it **red**. Feedback is shown immediately below the map.
    5. Placed chips (correct or incorrect) are removed from the strip. Continue until all 10 states are placed.
    6. Your final score is shown as **X / 10 correct**.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Heritage Site Builder — jigsaw puzzle">
    **Difficulty:** Medium

    Reassemble a scrambled monument image to reveal the complete heritage site.

    **How to play:**

    1. A random monument image (from states with monument images in the data) is selected and split into a **2×2 grid of 4 pieces**. The pieces are shuffled.
    2. Click one piece to select it (it highlights with an amber border). Then click a second piece to swap them.
    3. Repeat until the image is restored to its correct order (pieces 0, 1, 2, 3 from top-left to bottom-right).
    4. When the puzzle is solved, the complete image is revealed along with the **monument name** and **state**.
    5. Score is recorded as **1 / 1** on completion.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Guess the Dance Form — multiple choice">
    **Difficulty:** Medium

    Identify which Indian state a classical or folk dance form originates from.

    **How to play:**

    1. A dance form name is displayed — with an image when one is available.
    2. Choose which of the **4 listed states** the dance originates from.
    3. After selecting, immediate feedback is shown: your choice turns green (correct) or red (wrong), and the correct state is highlighted.
    4. Play continues for **10 rounds** drawn randomly from the performing arts data.
    5. Your final score is shown as **X / 10 correct**.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Historical Timeline — ordering game">
    **Difficulty:** Hard

    Arrange Indian historical periods in the correct chronological order from oldest to most recent.

    **How to play:**

    1. All historical periods from `timeline.json` are shuffled into a random order.
    2. Each period is displayed as a row with colored dot on a vertical timeline spine and **↑ / ↓ arrow buttons**.
    3. Click the **↑** or **↓** buttons to move a period up or down in your ordering.
    4. When you are satisfied with the order, click **Check order**.
    5. Each period placed in the correct chronological position scores 1 point.
    6. Correct entries turn **green**; incorrect entries turn **red**.
    7. Your final score is shown as **X / [total periods] correct**.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="State to Region — drag-and-drop classification">
    **Difficulty:** Medium

    Classify every Indian state into its correct geographic region.

    **How to play:**

    1. All Indian states appear as chips in a pool at the bottom of the screen.
    2. Six region boxes are shown in a grid: **North**, **South**, **East**, **West**, **Central**, **Northeast**.
    3. Drag a state chip and drop it onto the correct region box. On mobile, tap a chip to select it, then tap the target region box.
    4. Placed states appear inside the region box — green if correct, red if wrong.
    5. A **live progress bar** tracks how many states have been placed out of the total.
    6. The game ends when all states are placed. Your final score is shown as **X / [total states] correct**.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Monument & State — multiple choice">
    **Difficulty:** Easy

    Match famous heritage sites and monuments to the Indian state they are located in.

    **How to play:**

    1. A monument name is shown — with an image if one is available in the data.
    2. Choose which of the **4 listed states** the monument belongs to.
    3. Immediate feedback: your choice turns green (correct) or red (wrong), and the correct answer is highlighted.
    4. Play continues for **10 rounds** drawn randomly from the monument data.
    5. Your final score is shown as **X / 10 correct**.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Culture Trivia Quiz — multiple choice quiz">
    **Difficulty:** Hard

    Answer 10 multiple-choice questions drawn from a large bank of Indian culture, art, and heritage questions.

    **How to play:**

    1. 10 questions are selected randomly from the question bank in `src/data/quiz.json`.
    2. Each question shows a **category label** (e.g. Classical Dance, Monuments, Festivals) and the question text.
    3. Choose one of the **4 options (A / B / C / D)**. Your choice is highlighted green (correct) or red (wrong), and the correct answer is revealed.
    4. Click **Next** to advance to the following question.
    5. After 10 questions, your final score is shown as **X / 10 correct**.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Scores, certificates, and returning to the arcade

At the end of each game, your score is displayed in the format **X / Y correct**. You are then offered the option to generate a **Certificate of Participation**:

1. Confirm or update the name to appear on the certificate
2. Click to generate — the certificate is drawn on canvas with your name, game title, score, a unique ID, and the date
3. Click **Download** to save it as a PNG

Previously downloaded certificates are stored in your browser's `localStorage` so you can retrieve them without replaying the game.

Click **← Back to Arcade** to return to the game selection screen.
