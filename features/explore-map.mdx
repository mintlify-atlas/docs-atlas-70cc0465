The **Explore** page is the heart of Virasat. An interactive, D3-rendered map of India lets you navigate all 28 states and 8 union territories, filter by region, and dive into each state's cultural detail panel.

## How the map works

The map is built with D3's `geoMercator` projection and GeoJSON boundary data (`public/india-states.json`). It renders in SVG with smooth pan/zoom and click interactions powered by Framer Motion transitions.

## Opening a state

Click any state shape on the map to open its cultural detail panel. The map transitions to full-screen mode and a panel slides in from the right (on desktop) or up from the bottom (on mobile).

The state detail panel includes:
- **Monuments** — historical sites, temples, forts, and architectural wonders
- **Art Forms** — paintings, performing arts, and handicrafts
- **Fairs & Festivals** — annual cultural celebrations
- **Museums** — state museums and galleries

<Tip>
  You can deep-link directly to any state. For example, visit `/explore/Rajasthan` to immediately open Rajasthan's cultural panel. This URL is shareable and bookmarkable.
</Tip>

## Map interactions

- **Click a state** → opens the state detail panel
- **Click a region tab** → highlights states in that region
- **Eye icon** → toggles high-contrast color mode for accessibility
- **Scroll down on the page** → the map card animates from a rounded card to full-screen using Framer Motion scroll transforms
- **Close button / back** → returns to the full map view

<Note>
  The map uses a fluid expansion animation driven by Framer Motion's `useScroll` and `useTransform` hooks. As you scroll past the hero section, the map expands from a rounded, padded card to a borderless full-screen view.
</Note>

## 3D mudra showcase

At the top of the Explore page, before the map, is an "Ancient Hasta Mudras" showcase card showing 6 curated 3D mudra models in a horizontally scrollable row:

- **Alapadma** (single-hand) — fully bloomed lotus
- **Anjali** (two-hand) — salutation/namaste
- **Pataka** (single-hand) — flag gesture
- **Garuda** (two-hand) — eagle, Vishnu's vehicle
- **Mayura** (single-hand) — peacock
- **Chakra** (two-hand) — disc/wheel

Each model is interactive — drag to rotate, scroll to zoom.
