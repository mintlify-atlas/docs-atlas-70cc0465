The **Timeline** page (`/timeline`) presents India's history as an interactive horizontal scroll experience. Each historical era is a node on a continuous timeline line, and clicking any era expands it to reveal key events and highlights.

## How the timeline works

- **Horizontal scroll** — the timeline extends horizontally; scroll left/right to move through history
- **Era nodes** — large white dots represent major historical periods
- **Event nodes** — smaller dots represent specific events within an era (visible after expanding the era)
- **Click to expand** — clicking an era node expands it inline to a wide detail panel
- **Click again to collapse** — clicking the active item collapses it back to the timeline

<Note>
  On desktop, the expanded panel is 800px wide and shows a two-column layout with an image on the left and text on the right. On mobile, it stacks vertically.
</Note>

## Historical periods

The timeline covers 7 major periods of Indian history sourced from NCERT curriculum:

| Period | Years | Key themes |
|--------|-------|------------|
| **Pre-Historic** | Until 3000 BCE | Cave paintings, rock shelters, primitive tools, Bhimbetka |
| **Proto-Historic** | 3000–600 BCE | Indus Valley Civilization, Harappa, Mohenjo-daro, Dholavira |
| **Ancient** | 600 BCE–750 CE | Mauryas, Guptas, Buddhism, Jainism, Ashoka, Ajanta caves |
| **Early Medieval** | 750–1550 CE | Ellora Caves, Qutub Minar, Rajput kingdoms, Chola empire |
| **Medieval** | 1550–1750 CE | Mughal Empire, Basilica of Bom Jesus, Meenakshi Temple, Bundi miniatures |
| **Modern** | 1750–1980 CE | British rule, independence movement, Victoria Memorial, India Gate |
| **Contemporary** | 1980–Present | Lotus Temple, Manipuri Dance revival, post-independence culture |

## Navigating the timeline

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Timeline page">
    Navigate to `/timeline` from the main navigation bar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Scroll horizontally">
    Swipe left/right on mobile or use your mouse wheel / trackpad to scroll through the timeline. A styled amber scrollbar appears for mouse users.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click an era node">
    Click any large white circle (era node) to expand it. The node expands into a wide detail panel showing the era's image, years, title, and description. Sub-events also appear as smaller cards above or below the timeline line.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Explore sub-events">
    Click any sub-event card (smaller nodes) within an expanded era to see its specific detail panel — including image, location, and description.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Collapse and move on">
    Click the close button (×) in the top-right of the expanded panel to collapse it and continue scrolling.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Example highlights

Each era has curated highlight events. For example:

**Pre-Historic era highlights:**
- Bhimbetka Rock Shelters (Madhya Pradesh) — UNESCO World Heritage site with prehistoric rock paintings dating back 30,000 years

**Proto-Historic era highlights:**
- Dholavira (Gujarat) — one of the five largest Harappan sites, known for its sophisticated water management system
- Rakhigarhi (Haryana) — largest Indus Valley Civilization site in India

**Ancient era highlights:**
- Lion Capital of Ashoka (Sarnath, Uttar Pradesh) — the national emblem of India, originally placed atop an Ashoka pillar

## Image handling

Timeline images are served from the `public/time-line-images/` directory as `.webp` files. If a local image is unavailable, the `getTimelineFallbackImage()` utility provides a contextual placeholder.

<Tip>
  The timeline title header automatically collapses when you select an item — this gives the expanded panel maximum vertical space. It reappears when you close the item.
</Tip>
